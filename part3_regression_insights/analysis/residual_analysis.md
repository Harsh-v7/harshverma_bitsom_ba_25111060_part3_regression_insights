## Business Interpretation
- **Positive residuals** (under-predicted): These are stores performing better than what their input variables would suggest. This could point to unmodeled strengths — a stronger local brand presence, more effective management, or a loyal customer base.
- **Negative residuals** (over-predicted): These stores are falling short relative to the resources available to them. Likely explanations include weaker execution, intense local competition, or external disruptions the data doesn't capture.

## Patterns Observed
- Stores that are under-predicted tend to sit in regions with strong consumer demand.
- Stores that are over-predicted often have high inventory availability paired with weaker actual sales — pointing toward demand-side challenges rather than supply constraints.

## Limitations
- The residual analysis assumes the regression model itself is correctly specified.
- Stores with unusually large residuals may be better addressed through individual investigation rather than adjustments to the overall model.
